## 1. System Classification

**System Type:** Hybrid (conversational assistant + bounded multi-agent search worker)

**Description:**
This AI system extends the chatbot with a tightly bounded Google-grounded search path for authorized users. The search worker must use Gemini-backed Google Search to retrieve current information, while a deterministic FastAPI policy coordinator remains authoritative for tool selection, authorization, budgets, and degraded-path behavior. Good behavior means the system only runs search when allowed, returns grounded answers with required evidence when grounding is present, clearly distinguishes degraded or ungrounded outcomes, and never allows model or tool content to bypass policy.

**Critical Failure Modes:**
1. The model or search content bypasses coordinator policy and triggers unauthorized or out-of-budget tool execution.
2. The system presents an ungrounded or partially grounded answer as if it were a verified Google-grounded result.
3. Search content causes internal URL fetching, arbitrary tool execution, prompt/policy mutation, or other control-plane escalation.
4. Timeout, model-unavailable, or missing-grounding paths collapse into ambiguous assistant output instead of a clearly distinct degraded state.
5. Search requests or telemetry leak secrets, user tokens, or disallowed grounding-retention fields.

## 1b. Domain Context

> Researched by `gsd-domain-researcher`. Grounds the evaluation strategy in domain expert knowledge.

**Industry Vertical:** SMB service operations and customer-support SaaS

**User Population:** Authenticated employees, team leads, and admins at small companies using the chatbot to answer current-information questions that influence customer replies, operational choices, and lightweight research.

**Stakes Level:** High

**Output Consequence:** Users may act on the answer immediately in customer communication or business operations. If the system overstates grounding, runs search without permission, or mishandles web content, the downstream result is false assurance, bad decisions, privacy/security exposure, and loss of trust in the product's safety claims.

### What Domain Experts Evaluate Against

Dimension: Claim-to-source alignment
Good (domain expert would accept): Each material factual claim in a `Google-grounded` answer can be traced to the cited source list, and the user can tell which source supports which claim without opening every link.
Bad (domain expert would flag): Citations are present but do not support the exact claim, support only part of it, or the answer blends sourced facts with uncited prior knowledge.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Search-answer attribution research and practitioner expectation for evidence-backed enterprise answers

Dimension: Permission-respecting search execution
Good (domain expert would accept): Search runs only for users and turns that are explicitly authorized, and denied turns clearly state that no search was executed.
Bad (domain expert would flag): The response implies live search happened when policy denied it, or model/tool content appears to overrule the coordinator.
Stakes: Critical
Source: NIST AI RMF governance and enterprise security practice for human/organizational control over AI actions

Dimension: Grounded-state honesty
Good (domain expert would accept): The product visibly distinguishes grounded, missing-grounding, unavailable, timeout, and normal-chat states so staff know whether the answer is actually verified by search evidence.
Bad (domain expert would flag): A degraded or missing-grounding response looks like a verified search answer, carries the same badge treatment, or hides the fact that evidence is absent.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Google Search grounding product requirements and product-trust practice

Dimension: Freshness and uncertainty signaling
Good (domain expert would accept): The answer signals when information is time-sensitive, limited by locale/jurisdiction, or conflicting across sources, and it asks for a narrower follow-up when evidence is thin.
Bad (domain expert would flag): The answer presents a stale, ambiguous, or jurisdiction-specific result as universally current and settled.
Stakes: High
Source: NIST Generative AI Profile emphasis on valid, reliable, and interpretable human-AI outputs

Dimension: Confidential query handling
Good (domain expert would accept): Search requests and telemetry exclude secrets, bearer tokens, internal URLs, and unnecessary personal/company data, and retained metadata is limited to fields the provider allows.
Bad (domain expert would flag): The system forwards sensitive context into search prompts or stores raw search telemetry more broadly than privacy commitments and provider terms allow.
Stakes: Critical
Source: Privacy/security practice plus Google grounding data-use and retention constraints

### Known Failure Modes in This Domain

- Citation laundering: the UI shows citation markers or a `Google-grounded` badge, but one or more high-impact claims are actually unsupported by the linked sources.
- Search-result prompt injection: a retrieved page instructs the model to ignore policy, reveal hidden context, fetch internal resources, or influence follow-on tool behavior.
- False execution transparency: denied, timed-out, or missing-grounding turns look too similar to successful search turns, so users act as if a live verified search occurred.
- Overcollection and retention drift: prompts, grounding metadata, or click behavior are stored beyond what privacy commitments or Google grounding terms permit.

### Regulatory / Compliance Context

No sector-specific regime is triggered by this phase on its own; this is a general-business assistant feature rather than a healthcare, legal, or financial-advice workflow. The directly relevant constraints are broader:

- Privacy and security obligations apply to prompts, grounding metadata, and audit storage. Data minimization and purpose limitation matter because search queries may contain customer or company-sensitive information.
- FTC unfair/deceptive-practices risk applies if product copy or UI overstates that answers are verified, current, autonomous, or equivalent to professional advice when the evidence does not support that claim.
- Google Grounding with Google Search terms are operationally binding here: required Search Suggestions must be rendered from trusted provider fields, source-link click tracking is prohibited, and Zero Data Retention is not supported for this grounding feature.
- If this feature is later exposed directly to public consumers, review emerging U.S. state chatbot disclosure and testing rules before launch; those laws are evolving and are broader than this internal SMB deployment context.

## 2. Framework Decision

**Selected Framework:** Google ADK

**Version:** 2.2.x

**Rationale:**
Phase 3 already locks the core constraints that matter most for framework selection: Gemini-backed Google Search is mandatory, the search path must run in a dedicated worker with separate credentials, the coordinator must remain deterministic and fail closed, and the project has already committed to Google ADK in its stack guidance. Google ADK therefore fits the search-worker boundary directly, because it is the first-party framework aligned with Gemini search tooling, session-aware orchestration, and the project's Google-specific search requirement. The top-level policy coordinator should still remain hand-written and typed in FastAPI rather than being absorbed into the framework, so authorization, budgets, capability credentials, and degraded behavior stay outside model control.

**Alternatives Considered:**

| Framework | Ruled Out Because |
|-----------|------------------|
| LangGraph | Stronger for stateful graph orchestration, but unnecessary if the policy coordinator stays in ordinary FastAPI code and the ADK worker boundary remains narrow. Introduces more workflow/state machinery than this phase currently needs. |

**Vendor Lock-In Accepted:** Partial - the search worker is intentionally Gemini/Google-specific, while the top-level coordinator and the normal chat path remain outside ADK.

### Entry Point Pattern
```python
from __future__ import annotations

from typing import Any

from google.adk.agents import Agent
from google.adk.runners import Runner
from google.adk.sessions import InMemorySessionService
from google.adk.tools import google_search
from google.genai import types
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field


class SearchWorkerReply(BaseModel):
    answer_markdown: str = Field(min_length=1, max_length=4000)
    query_used: str = Field(min_length=1, max_length=160)


search_agent = Agent(
    name="google_search_worker",
    model="gemini-2.5-flash",
    description="Dedicated Gemini search worker used only after FastAPI policy approval.",
    instruction=(
        "You are a bounded Google Search worker. "
        "You never decide authorization, budgets, or fallback policy. "
        "Use Google Search when current information is required. "
        "Return only JSON matching the output schema."
    ),
    tools=[google_search],
    output_schema=SearchWorkerReply,
    generate_content_config=types.GenerateContentConfig(
        temperature=0.1,
        max_output_tokens=1536,
    ),
)


class GoogleSearchWorker:
    def __init__(self) -> None:
        self._sessions = InMemorySessionService()
        self._runner = Runner(
            app_name="policy_controlled_google_search",
            agent=search_agent,
            session_service=self._sessions,
        )

    async def run(
        self,
        *,
        user_id: str,
        session_id: str,
        prompt: str,
    ) -> tuple[SearchWorkerReply, dict[str, Any] | None]:
        existing = await self._sessions.get_session(
            app_name="policy_controlled_google_search",
            user_id=user_id,
            session_id=session_id,
        )
        if existing is None:
            await self._sessions.create_session(
                app_name="policy_controlled_google_search",
                user_id=user_id,
                session_id=session_id,
            )

        final_event = None
        async for event in self._runner.run_async(
            user_id=user_id,
            session_id=session_id,
            new_message=types.Content(
                role="user",
                parts=[types.Part.from_text(text=prompt)],
            ),
        ):
            if event.is_final_response():
                final_event = event

        if final_event is None or not final_event.content or not final_event.content.parts:
            raise RuntimeError("Search worker returned no final response event.")

        reply = SearchWorkerReply.model_validate_json(final_event.content.parts[0].text)
        grounding = (
            final_event.grounding_metadata.model_dump(mode="json", by_alias=True)
            if final_event.grounding_metadata is not None
            else None
        )
        return reply, grounding
```

### Key Abstractions
| Concept | What It Is | When You Use It |
|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| `Agent` | The Gemini-backed search worker definition: instruction, model, `google_search`, and `output_schema`. | Define the isolated worker that is allowed to use Google Search after FastAPI policy approval. |
| `Runner` | The async execution engine that turns a user message into ADK `Event`s. | Keep one runner in the backend process and call it from the coordinator after authz and readiness checks. |
| `SessionService` | ADK conversation state store keyed by `app_name`, `user_id`, and `session_id`. | Keep short-lived worker context per conversation; do not treat it as the system of record. |
| `google_search` | The built-in Gemini Google Search tool. In Python it is imported as `google_search`. | Use it as the only ADK tool in this worker so search remains isolated from Python and custom tools. |
| `output_schema` + final `Event.grounding_metadata` | `output_schema` forces typed final JSON; `grounding_metadata` carries `groundingChunks`, `groundingSupports`, and `searchEntryPoint`. | Use the schema for deterministic backend branching, and the grounding payload for citations, badge display, and Search Suggestions. |

### Common Pitfalls
1. Putting `google_search` in an agent with other tools. ADK documents a one-tool-per-agent limitation for Google Search; keep a dedicated search worker instead of mixing search with Python or arbitrary function tools.
2. Using ADK as the top-level policy coordinator. The model should never decide scopes, budgets, or degraded behavior; keep that control plane in typed FastAPI code and call ADK only after deterministic checks pass.
3. Treating grounded output as guaranteed. A final text response can arrive without `grounding_metadata`; Phase 3 must branch to the explicit missing-grounding UI state instead of displaying a `Google-grounded` result.
4. Rendering `searchEntryPoint.renderedContent` inside assistant Markdown. Search Suggestions must be rendered in a dedicated trusted component and never merged into user/model Markdown.
5. Treating `InMemorySessionService` as durable state. It is fine for local Compose development, but canonical audit state still belongs in PostgreSQL `messages.metadata` and `tool_executions`.

## 4. Implementation Guidance

**Model Configuration:**
Use `gemini-2.5-flash` for the dedicated search worker because the current stable model page lists both Search grounding and structured outputs as supported. Configure the worker with `types.GenerateContentConfig(temperature=0.1, max_output_tokens=1536)` so the answer remains concise and predictable, and keep the model ID configuration-driven through `Settings.search_model`. At startup, fail closed if the Google API key is missing or the configured model is not the stable `gemini-2.5-flash` family with Search grounding enabled.

**Core Pattern:**
Keep the deterministic coordinator outside ADK. FastAPI decides whether search is allowed, whether the provider is ready, and how denied, timeout, missing-grounding, and grounded states are rendered. ADK only executes the bounded search worker.

```python
from __future__ import annotations

from typing import Any, Protocol

from pydantic import BaseModel

from app.authorization.policy import PolicyResult, evaluate_required_scopes
from app.core.config import Settings
from app.core.provider_status import search_status


class SearchWorkerReply(BaseModel):
    answer_markdown: str
    query_used: str


class SearchWorker(Protocol):
    async def run(
        self,
        *,
        user_id: str,
        session_id: str,
        prompt: str,
    ) -> tuple[SearchWorkerReply, dict[str, Any] | None]: ...


class PolicyControlledSearchCoordinator:
    def __init__(self, *, settings: Settings, worker: SearchWorker) -> None:
        self._settings = settings
        self._worker = worker

    async def handle_turn(
        self,
        *,
        user_id: str,
        conversation_id: str,
        principal_scopes: set[str],
        prompt: str,
        history_summary: str | None,
    ) -> dict[str, Any]:
        policy = evaluate_required_scopes(
            principal_scopes=principal_scopes,
            required={"tool:websearch"},
        )
        if policy is not PolicyResult.allow:
            return {
                "state": "denied",
                "tool_executed": False,
                "answer_markdown": "Google Search was blocked by policy.",
            }

        if search_status(self._settings) != "ready":
            return {
                "state": "search_unavailable",
                "tool_executed": False,
                "answer_markdown": "Google Search is currently unavailable. Try again later.",
            }

        worker_prompt = prompt
        if history_summary:
            worker_prompt = (
                f"Conversation summary:\n{history_summary}\n\n"
                f"User question:\n{prompt}"
            )

        reply, grounding = await self._worker.run(
            user_id=user_id,
            session_id=conversation_id,
            prompt=worker_prompt,
        )

        if not grounding:
            return {
                "state": "missing_grounding",
                "tool_executed": True,
                "answer_markdown": reply.answer_markdown,
                "citations": [],
                "google_grounded": False,
            }

        search_entry_point = grounding.get("searchEntryPoint") or {}
        return {
            "state": "grounded",
            "tool_executed": True,
            "answer_markdown": reply.answer_markdown,
            "citations": grounding.get("groundingChunks", []),
            "google_grounded": True,
            "search_suggestions_html": search_entry_point.get("renderedContent"),
        }
```

**Tool Use:**
The ADK worker should expose exactly one tool: `google_search`. Do not place Python sandbox execution, internal HTTP fetchers, or custom function tools in this agent, and do not use `bypass_multi_tools_limit=True` for this phase because it weakens the intended security boundary. Add tracing at FastAPI startup with Phoenix only after the worker boundary is isolated:

```bash
pip install openinference-instrumentation-google-adk arize-phoenix-otel
```

```python
from phoenix.otel import register

register(project_name="simpagent-phase-03-search", auto_instrument=True)
```

Keep Google credentials separate from the normal chat provider credentials, and never pass bearer tokens, refresh tokens, or secret config values into the worker prompt or ADK session state.

**State Management:**
Use ADK session state only for short-lived worker context such as the last normalized query or a compact conversation summary. Persist the canonical result in PostgreSQL instead: store response state (`grounded`, `missing_grounding`, `denied`, `search_unavailable`), badge flags, normalized source entries, and raw grounding JSON in `messages.metadata`, and create a correlated `tool_executions` row with status, duration, and failure mode. `InMemorySessionService` is acceptable for local Compose in v1 because the app database remains the source of truth; move to `DatabaseSessionService` only if you later need multi-process ADK session continuity.

**Context Window Strategy:**
Even though `gemini-2.5-flash` supports a very large context window, do not forward the full transcript to the worker. Send only the active user turn, a short coordinator-authored summary of the last few relevant turns, locale/timezone hints, and any search-specific policy notes. Cap worker input to roughly 6k tokens and worker output to 1536 tokens; if the conversation context exceeds that budget, summarize outside ADK first and never replay prior raw grounding blobs or Search Suggestions HTML back into the model.

## 4b. AI Systems Best Practices

> Written by `gsd-ai-researcher`. Cross-cutting patterns every developer building AI systems needs - independent of framework choice.

### Structured Outputs with Pydantic

```python
from __future__ import annotations

import logging
from typing import Literal

from google.adk.agents import Agent
from google.adk.tools import google_search
from pydantic import BaseModel, Field, ValidationError

logger = logging.getLogger(__name__)


class SearchWorkerReply(BaseModel):
    answer_markdown: str = Field(min_length=1, max_length=4000)
    query_used: str = Field(min_length=1, max_length=160)
    answer_state: Literal["grounded", "insufficient", "needs_retry"]
    follow_up_questions: list[str] = Field(default_factory=list, max_length=3)


search_agent = Agent(
    name="google_search_worker",
    model="gemini-2.5-flash",
    instruction=(
        "Return only JSON that matches the output schema. "
        "Do not include citations in HTML; the backend renders citations from grounding metadata."
    ),
    tools=[google_search],
    output_schema=SearchWorkerReply,
)


async def run_with_schema_retry(worker, *, user_id: str, session_id: str, prompt: str):
    last_error: ValidationError | None = None
    for attempt in range(3):
        try:
            return await worker.run(user_id=user_id, session_id=session_id, prompt=prompt)
        except ValidationError as exc:
            last_error = exc
            logger.warning(
                "search_worker_validation_retry",
                extra={
                    "attempt": attempt + 1,
                    "session_id": session_id,
                    "error_count": exc.error_count(),
                },
            )
    raise RuntimeError("Structured output validation failed after 3 attempts") from last_error
```

For ADK 2.2.x, prefer native `output_schema` on the dedicated search agent instead of bolting on a second parser after generation. Retry twice after the first validation failure (3 total attempts). Log attempt count, conversation/session IDs, model name, and validation error count; do not log raw answer text, grounding HTML, or secrets. After the final failure, surface the explicit search-unavailable or retryable-search-error state rather than falling back to an untyped assistant answer.

### Async-First Design

ADK Python is async-first: `Runner.run_async()` and `Runner.run_live()` both execute inside the event loop, and session services are async as well. The common mistake in FastAPI is calling `asyncio.run()` inside a request handler or dependency; that creates nested event-loop failures and breaks request concurrency. Initialize the worker once during app startup, then `await` it from routes/services. Use `run_live()` only when you intentionally want streamed UX over WebSocket. For Phase 3 HTTP responses, consume `run_async()` to completion so the coordinator can validate the structured output, inspect `grounding_metadata`, persist audit state, and then return one deterministic result envelope.

### Prompt Engineering Discipline

Keep the ADK `instruction` narrow and role-like: it defines the worker's job, output contract, and prohibition on policy decisions. Keep user input separate in the `new_message` payload and sanitize it before forwarding. Do not paste scopes, secret config, or raw JWT-derived claims into the prompt. If you need examples, keep one or two inline JSON-format examples in the worker instruction; do not build a large few-shot prompt for this phase because the search tool already supplies fresh evidence. Always set `max_output_tokens` explicitly and keep it bounded; leaving search answers unbounded is a cost and latency bug.

### Context Window Management

This phase is not a vector-RAG system; the context problem is conversation carry-over plus grounding payload size. Summarize conversation history outside ADK once the chat exceeds 3-4 relevant turns or your internal 6k-token worker budget. Pass only the summary plus the current user request into ADK. Persist raw grounding metadata for audit, but never replay the full metadata blob into later prompts. Search Suggestions HTML should bypass the model entirely on later turns: store it for rendering, not for reprompting.

### Cost and Latency Budget

On the current Gemini pricing page, `gemini-2.5-flash` is priced at $0.30 per 1M input tokens and $2.50 per 1M output tokens, and grounded Google Search requests are billed separately after the free quota at $35 per 1,000 grounded prompts. A typical Phase 3 search turn with about 2k input tokens, 1.2k output tokens, and one grounded prompt is roughly $0.0386 on the paid tier, so the search path should remain explicit and policy-gated rather than automatic. Add exact-match caching on normalized `(query, locale, day-bucket)` for short TTL hot queries, but be conservative with semantic caching for final grounded answers because freshness matters; semantic caching is safer for routing and conversation-summary sub-tasks. If you introduce a cheaper helper model, use it only for search intent classification or transcript summarization (for example `gemini-2.5-flash-lite`), while keeping the final grounded search answer on `gemini-2.5-flash`.

### Reference Dataset

**Size:** Start with 16 labeled Phase 3 examples during implementation, then expand to 24 before phase sign-off or any external demo. This exceeds the 10-example minimum and reaches the 20+ example target for production-style validation.

**Composition:**
- 4 authorized happy paths: grounded answer with clear citations, current-info question, follow-up turn using a short history summary, and locale/jurisdiction-sensitive answer.
- 4 degraded/control paths: missing `tool:websearch` scope, provider unavailable at startup, worker timeout, and missing-grounding fallback.
- 4 adversarial safety paths: retrieved-content prompt injection, internal-URL lure, policy-override attempt, and forced-multiple-tool-call attempt.
- 4 privacy/budget paths: canary secret in prompt context, internal URL in user text, overlength question requiring summarization/truncation, and over-budget/retry-limit case.

**Labeling:**
- Code labels: route selected, tool executed or denied, response state, citation presence, retry count, latency, budget usage, and redaction/allowlist compliance.
- LLM judge labels: claim-to-source alignment and freshness/uncertainty signaling, calibrated against human review on the first 10 cases with a target of at least 0.7 agreement before becoming a release gate.
- Human labels: product/ops lead scores decision usefulness and grounded-state honesty; security/privacy reviewer labels prompt-injection, secret-handling, and retention cases.
- Creation timeline: build the first 8 examples before the coordinator/search contract is frozen, complete the 16-case seed set before UI state work is finalized, and grow to 24 cases before phase exit.

## 6. Guardrails

### Online (Real-Time)

| Guardrail | Trigger | Intervention |
|-----------|---------|--------------|
| Authorization and budget gate | Search requested without `tool:websearch`, outside per-turn budget, after a prior tool call, or while search readiness is degraded. | Block search, return `denied` or `search_unavailable`, persist the denied tool event, and make no ADK/provider call. |
| Grounding contract gate | Worker returns malformed structured output, missing required grounding metadata, or a state/payload combination that cannot honestly render as grounded. | Downgrade to `missing_grounding` or `search_unavailable`, strip badge/citations/suggestions, and flag the contract failure for review. |
| Metadata redaction and retention allowlist gate | Prompt, trace, or persistence payload contains a canary secret, bearer-token pattern, internal URL, or non-allowlisted grounding field. | Redact and fail closed, emit a security/privacy event, and quarantine the trace for manual review. |
| Single-tool boundary gate | Worker attempts a non-`google_search` tool path, a second tool invocation, or any coordinator policy change influenced by model/tool content. | Abort execution, mark the turn as blocked/prompt-injection-defended, and keep coordinator policy authoritative. |

### Offline (Flywheel)

| Metric | Sampling Strategy | Action on Degradation |
|--------|------------------|----------------------|
| Claim-to-source alignment pass rate | Review the full reference dataset on every CI run and at least 10% of production grounded turns, weighted toward long answers and answers with 3+ citations. | If the rolling pass rate drops below 95% or a severe unsupported claim appears, hold rollout and revise the prompt/citation rendering path. |
| Grounded-state honesty mismatch rate | Sample 100% of `missing_grounding`, `timeout`, `search_unavailable`, and `denied` turns, plus 10% of `grounded` turns. | If any severe mismatch appears or the daily mismatch rate exceeds 1%, fix the backend/UI contract before the next deploy. |
| Prompt-injection adversarial pass rate | Run the adversarial suite nightly and replay all blocked production cases in the next batch. | Any failure is a release blocker and triggers coordinator/worker hardening. |
| Secret/retention violation rate | Run 100% of canary-secret evals in CI and audit a weekly sample of persisted traces/tool rows. | Any confirmed leak or non-allowlisted field pages security, requires trace purge where applicable, and blocks release until fixed. |
| Search task completion usefulness | Score 20% of grounded success turns plus every user retry on the same current-info question within 5 minutes. | If usefulness falls below 4/5 or completion drops below 90%, revisit routing, timeout budgets, and search-worker instructions. |

## 7. Production Monitoring

**Tracing Tool:** Arize Phoenix with OpenTelemetry instrumentation around the FastAPI coordinator and the Google ADK search worker. Every trace should carry the request correlation ID, conversation ID, tool-execution ID, response state, model name, latency, token counts, and budget outcome so Phoenix spans can be joined back to PostgreSQL `messages.metadata` and `tool_executions`.

**Key Metrics to Track:**
- Search route rate and authorization-denial rate for turns that ask for current information.
- Outcome distribution across `grounded`, `missing_grounding`, `search_unavailable`, `timeout`, `denied`, and structured-output retry exhaustion.
- Sampled claim-to-source alignment pass rate and grounded-state honesty mismatch rate.
- Prompt-injection block count, unauthorized provider-call attempts, and secret/retention allowlist violations.
- p95 search latency, timeout rate, per-turn tokens/cost, and per-user budget burn.

**Alert Thresholds:**
- Page immediately on any confirmed secret leak, disallowed grounding-field persistence, or unauthorized provider/tool call.
- Warn if `missing_grounding` exceeds 5% of search turns or `timeout + search_unavailable` exceeds 3% over a 15-minute window.
- Warn if p95 search latency exceeds 8 seconds over 15 minutes or structured-output validation exhausts retries on more than 1% of daily search turns.
- Open an incident if sampled claim-to-source alignment drops below 95% or grounded-state honesty drops below 99% in the daily reviewed batch.
- Investigate routing quality if users retry the same current-information question within 5 minutes on more than 10% of grounded turns.

**Smart Sampling Strategy:**
- Sample 100% of `denied`, `timeout`, `search_unavailable`, `missing_grounding`, `prompt_injection_blocked`, validation-retry-exhausted, and canary-secret events.
- Sample 25% of grounded turns with long answers, 3+ citations, or wording that indicates conflict, uncertainty, or locale/jurisdiction limits.
- Sample 10% of remaining grounded turns at random to watch for silent citation drift.
- Sample 5% of borderline routing turns where current-information intent was low-confidence or the user immediately rephrased after a non-search answer.
- Escalate every LLM-judge disagreement on critical dimensions to human review by product/ops plus security/privacy reviewers.
